Efficient L1-2 Regularized Logistic Regression

Machine Learning

Role
Researcher
Task
1. we proposed an L1-2 regularized logistic regression model together with an efficient algorithm to solve it.
2. Our algorithm is based on difference of convex functions algorithm(DCA) and alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM), which iteratively linearizes the L2 penalty term and use DCA and quadratic approximation to solve an L1 regularized sub-problem.
Achievement
Our trials on both simulated data sets and real data sets show that our proposed method performs significantly better than the standard L1 and L2 regularized logistic regression and produces sparser solutions. Some Resustls and comparison are shown in the following pictures.

Simulated Gaussian Dataset

We compared our proposed method with L1 regularized logistic regression and both the primal and dual problems of L2 regularized logistic regression on a Gaussian simulated data set. The results the both data set show that our proposed method has a better performance.

Ionosphere Dataset

ionosphere data set collected by the Applied Physics Laboratory at John Hopkins University, which could be found at the following url: http://archive.ics.uci.edu/ml/datasets/Ionosphere.

Our method is compared to the L1 regularized logistic regression and both the primal and dual problems of L2 regularized logistic regression implemented by the liblinear library. As table shows, our method performs significantly better than the other methods. While L1 and L2 regularized logistic regression do a poor job in capturing the pattern of ionosphere radar returns, our method maintains adecent accuracy with its ROC curve area twice those of the other methods.
